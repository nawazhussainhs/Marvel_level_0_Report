# Introduction to PCB Design

## What is a PCB?

A **Printed Circuit Board (PCB)** is used to mechanically support and electrically connect electronic components using copper traces instead of wires. PCBs are found in devices like mobile phones, laptops, embedded systems, and power supplies.

---

## PCB Design Process

1. **Schematic Design** – Create the circuit diagram using software such as KiCad.
2. **Assign Footprints** – Map schematic symbols to their physical component packages.
3. **PCB Layout** – Place components and route copper traces.
4. **Design Rule Check (DRC)** – Verify spacing, clearance, and connectivity rules.
5. **Generate Gerber Files** – Create manufacturing files for PCB fabrication.

---

## Key PCB Design Concepts

- **Trace Width** – Determines current carrying capacity. Higher current requires wider traces.
- **Ground Plane** – Reduces noise and improves signal stability.
- **Vias** – Connect different layers in multi-layer PCBs.
- **Decoupling Capacitors** – Placed near IC power pins to reduce voltage spikes.

---

## Conclusion

PCB design is a fundamental skill in electronics engineering. A well-designed PCB ensures reliability, reduced noise, proper heat management, and efficient circuit performance. It bridges the gap between theoretical circuit design and real-world hardware implementation.
